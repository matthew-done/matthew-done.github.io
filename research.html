Parisi-Sourlas Quantisation of Constrained Hamiltonian Systems - 2024-2025

My final year project in Trinity College Dublin where I researched an alternative method for quantising field theories with first and second class constraints in the canoncial formalism. The project involved aspects from classical and quantum field theory, gauge theory, supersymmetry and symplectic geometry.

My research began with a detailed study of classical contrained systems in the canonical formalism based on the Dirac-Bergmann algorithm. This was followed by a review of the BRST formalism which was originally discovered by researchers who were attempting to quantise gauge theories, particularly pure Yang-Mills theory. The goal of my project was to introduce the Parisi-Sourlas formalism and show how it generalises the BRST formalism to a method which can be applied to quantise systems with both first and second class constraints - the BRST formalism can only be applied to first class constraints, or a system of second class constraints which have been converted to effective first class constraints using, for example, the BFT embedding method. Using the non-Abelian pure Yang-Mills theory as a toy model I was able to show that the Parisi-Sourlas construction successfully leads to a covariant path-integral formulated over an extended phase space which is formally equivalent to the path-integral of the original system by virtue of the Parisi-Sourlas supersymmetry. Additionally, the known BRST charge of pure Yang-Mills theory was recovered as the localised expression of a specific generator of Parisi-Sourlas transformations.
